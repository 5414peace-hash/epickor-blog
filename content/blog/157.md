So you have one week in South Korea. How do you see it all? From the neon-lit skyscrapers of Seoul to the historical soul of Gyeongju and the coastal energy of Busan, this **7-Day Itinerary** is designed to give you the ultimate "Best of Korea" experience without burning you out.

### 1. Days 1-3: The Heart of Seoul
- **Day 1:** Explore **Gyeongbokgung Palace** in a Hanbok, then head to **Myeongdong** for street food.
- **Day 2:** Experience the "Hip" side in **Seongsu-dong** and catch the sunset at **Namsan Tower**.
- **Day 3:** A morning trip to the **DMZ** (Demilitarized Zone) to understand the modern history of the peninsula.

![A traveler in Hanbok walking through a palace courtyard](/assets/images/posts/157/157_01.jpg)![Night view of Myeongdong crowded with food stalls](/assets/images/posts/157/157_02.jpg)

### 2. Days 4-5: Gyeongju, the Museum Without Walls
Take the KTX (High-speed train) to **Gyeongju**.
- **Day 4:** Visit **Bulguksa Temple** and **Seokguram Grotto**, both UNESCO World Heritage sites.
- **Day 5:** Explore the ancient **Silla Tombs** and the stunning **Donggung Palace and Wolji Pond** at night.

![The iconic stone staircase of Bulguksa Temple](/assets/images/posts/157/157_03.jpg)![The reflection of Wolji Pond palace in the water at night](/assets/images/posts/157/157_04.jpg)

### 3. Days 6-7: The Coastal Energy of Busan
- **Day 6:** Head to **Haeundae Beach** and the colorful **Gamcheon Culture Village**.
- **Day 7:** Eat fresh seafood at **Jagalchi Market** before taking the KTX back to Seoul for your flight.

### FAQ: Travel Logistics
**Q: Should I get a Korea Rail Pass?**
A: If you are doing the Seoul-Gyeongju-Busan route, the KORAIL Pass is highly recommended and cost-effective.
**Q: Is 7 days enough?**
A: It's enough to see the highlights, but you'll definitely want to come back for more!
