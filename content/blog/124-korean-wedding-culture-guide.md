# The Ultimate Guide to Korean Wedding Culture: K-Drama Romance vs. Reality

In Korea, a wedding is more than just a union between two people; it's a grand merging of two family empires. While K-Dramas like *Queen of Tears* or *Hometown Cha-Cha-Cha* make it look purely romantic, the reality is a fascinating blend of deep-rooted tradition, high-stakes family negotiations, and ultra-modern commercial efficiency. 

If you are a foreigner invited to a Korean wedding or simply curious about the culture, this deep dive will reveal everything from the "Su-De-Me" holy trinity to the strict rules of congratulatory cash.

### 2. Invitations: Why a Text Message Isn't Enough

Next are the invitations. While "Mobile Invitations" via KakaoTalk are common for general acquaintances, the physical invitation remains a pillar of Korean social respect. 

Korean wedding invitations arenâ€™t just casual "save the date" cards. Traditionally, the couple meets their friends and colleagues in person to hand over the invitation over a meal. In Korea, "I'm getting married" usually translates to "I'm buying you dinner." Failing to provide a meal while giving an invitation can be seen as a breach of social contract.

### 3. The "Su-De-Me" Holy Trinity (Studio, Dress, Makeup)

For many Korean couples, **"Su-De-Me"** is the most expensive and time-consuming part of the prep. It stands for:
- **Studio:** Pre-wedding photoshoots that last 8-10 hours in professional studios with elaborate sets.
- **Dress:** Unlike in the West, most Korean brides *rent* several high-end designer dresses (often 3-4 different ones) for the shoot and the ceremony. 
- **Makeup:** Professional makeup artists transform the couple into royalty for the day.

### 4. The 60-Minute Marathon: Wedding Halls & Buffets

The big day in Korea is incredibly efficient. Most ceremonies take place in dedicated **"Wedding Halls"**â€”buildings designed solely to host weddings, often hosting multiple ceremonies per hour.

- **The Speed:** The main ceremony often lasts only 30 to 60 minutes. There is no time for long speeches; the couple walks down the aisle, bows to their parents, and immediately heads to the photo zone.
- **The Buffet:** The real highlight for guests is the buffet. In Korea, the reputation of a couple's wedding is often judged by the quality of the hall's food. Guests often skip the ceremony and head straight to the dining hall!

### 6. FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I wear?**
A: Business formal or smart casual. Avoid wearing white (reserved for the bride) or overly casual clothes like jeans.

**Q: Can I bring a plus-one?**
A: Only if the couple has explicitly invited them. Since wedding buffets are priced per head, unexpected guests can be a financial burden.

**Q: How long should I stay?**
A: Most guests stay for the ceremony and the meal, totaling about 1.5 to 2 hours.
